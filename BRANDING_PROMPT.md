## The Brief

I need a complete brand identity for **Cannabisers** — a B2B directory for the cannabis industry. This is a professional trade resource, not a consumer dispensary finder or stoner culture site. Think "Yellow Pages meets LinkedIn for cannabis businesses."

## What Cannabisers Is

A directory website where cannabis operators (dispensary owners, cultivators, processors) find B2B service providers: testing labs, compliance consultants, attorneys, packaging companies, insurance, HVAC, security, accounting, software, and 10+ other categories. Coverage across all 30+ legal US states.

**The tagline is:** "The Cannabis Industry Directory"

**The audience is:** Cannabis business operators and executives. These are serious business owners managing million-dollar operations in a heavily regulated industry. They need reliable vendors and they need them fast. They don't want to see tie-dye and pot leaves — they want to see credibility, professionalism, and comprehensive coverage.

**The competitive positioning is:** The only comprehensive, state-by-state B2B directory for cannabis ancillary services. Existing alternatives are either consumer-facing (Weedmaps, Leafly), media companies with directories bolted on (MJBizDaily), or tiny underfunded listings sites. We're the serious trade resource.

## What I Need

### 1. Color Palette (5-6 colors)

**Requirements:**
- Dark theme (dark backgrounds, light text). This matches the cannabis industry aesthetic and differentiates from sterile white directory sites.
- Primary color that signals "cannabis" without being cliché (no bright/lime green — that reads recreational/stoner). Think deep, sophisticated greens, teals, or earth tones.
- Accent color for CTAs, highlights, and interactive elements. Should pop against the dark background.
- Background color (very dark, not pure black)
- Card/surface color (slightly lighter than background)
- Text colors (primary white/light, secondary muted)
- Error/warning/success states

**Aesthetic references from my other sites:**
- AI Market Pulse: Dark teal #0f2d35 bg, gold #e8a87c accent — premium, analytical
- Fieldwork: Dark #14171C bg, ember/rust #E07850 accent — warm, professional
- ExecSignals: Dark navy #111827 bg, gold #D4A054 accent — executive, authoritative

I want something in that family but distinctly "cannabis industry" — professional, credible, not recreational.

### 2. Typography (2-3 fonts)

**Requirements:**
- Display/heading font: Strong, modern, professional. Used for page titles, section headers, hero text. Should feel authoritative — this is a trade directory, not a lifestyle blog.
- Body font: Clean, readable at small sizes. Used for listing descriptions, category text, meta content. Needs to work at 14-16px on desktop and mobile.
- Mono font (optional): For data elements, license numbers, stats, metadata. Adds a "data-driven" feel.
- All fonts must be available on Google Fonts (free, no licensing issues)

**Font references from my other sites:**
- Plus Jakarta Sans (body) — used on DataStackGuide, Provyx, ExecSignals. Clean and modern.
- DM Serif Display (headings) — used on ExecSignals. Magazine-quality authority.
- Space Mono / IBM Plex Mono (data) — used on DataStackGuide, Fieldwork.
- Sora (headings) — used on Fieldwork. Geometric, clean.

Feel free to suggest different fonts if they better fit the cannabis industry positioning, but don't pick anything decorative or playful. This is B2B.

### 3. Logo Concepts (3 options)

**Requirements:**
- The logo must work at small sizes (32x32 favicon, 160x44 header, 1200x630 social preview)
- Must include the text "Cannabisers" (no abbreviation)
- Optional icon/mark that works standalone as favicon
- Must look professional on dark backgrounds (transparent background + light text version needed)
- No pot leaves, no cannabis leaf icons, no marijuana imagery. The audience actively avoids cliché cannabis branding. The icon should be abstract or geometric — think directory/network/connection/grid metaphors.
- The logo should signal "directory" or "marketplace" or "professional network" — not "cannabis product"

**Logo format needed:**
- SVG preferred (vector, scalable)
- Horizontal layout (icon + text side by side) for header
- Icon-only version for favicon
- Both dark-bg (light text) and light-bg (dark text) versions

### 4. Visual Language Guidelines

Give me guidance on:
- Icon style for categories (outlined vs filled, rounded vs sharp)
- Card styling (borders, shadows, radius)
- Button styling (radius, padding, hover states)
- Badge/tag styling for categories and states
- Spacing rhythm (4px, 8px base?)
- How to handle state-level color coding or differentiation
- Photo/illustration approach (if any)

## What I Don't Want

- Anything that looks like a dispensary menu or cannabis delivery app
- Bright green (#00FF00 or similar) — this is the #1 cliché in cannabis branding
- Pot leaves, cannabis leaf icons, marijuana imagery of any kind
- "Stoner" aesthetic (tie-dye, psychedelic, trippy, neon)
- Overly corporate/enterprise aesthetic (we're not Salesforce). Should feel approachable but professional.
- Script/handwritten fonts
- Gradients as primary design elements (subtle gradients okay for CTAs)

## Deliverables

Please provide:
1. **Color palette** with hex codes, named by role (--color-bg, --color-surface, --color-primary, --color-accent, --color-text, --color-text-muted, etc.)
2. **Font stack** with Google Fonts names, weights to load, and where each is used (headings, body, data/mono)
3. **3 logo concepts** described in detail (I'll generate the actual SVGs separately, so describe the concept, shapes, proportions, and colors clearly)
4. **CSS custom properties** block I can drop into my stylesheet
5. **Visual language summary** (icon style, card style, button style, spacing)

Make it feel like the cannabis industry's Bloomberg Terminal — professional, data-rich, dark, authoritative. The kind of site where a dispensary owner feels confident finding their next compliance consultant or testing lab.
