Frequently Asked Questions

How must cannabis waste be disposed of?

Most states require cannabis waste to be rendered unrecognizable and unusable by mixing with non-cannabis material (soil, food waste, paper). The mixture must be at least 50% non-cannabis by weight. Waste manifests and destruction records must be maintained for 3-7 years.

What does cannabis waste disposal cost?

Pickup service runs $200-600 per visit for small operators. Monthly contracts for larger facilities cost $500-2,000. Some companies offer composting programs that reduce costs. Non-compliance fines start at $5,000 per violation, so the service pays for itself.

Can cannabis waste be composted?

Yes, in most states. Cannabis plant waste (stalks, leaves, roots) can be composted if properly mixed and rendered unusable first. Some states allow on-site composting with proper documentation. Concentrate and edible waste usually requires off-site destruction.
